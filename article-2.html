Hydrate Skin With These Hyaluronic Acid Rich Foods Skin Type

In the last few years, there have been a lot of fast growing skincare ingredients but the one that has been the most popular so far is Hyaluronic acid. HA helps hydrate your skin by holding in over 1000x its weight in water.

But, applying Hyaluronic acid directly to your face is not the only way! Hyaluronic acid foods and supplements can provide you with the Hyaluronic acid your skin needs.

List of foods containing hyaluronic acid – Some foods contain hyaluronic acid or substances necessary for the body’s production of hyaluronic acid. Hyaluronic acid binds moisture, tightens the connective tissue, and increases collagen production.

Now lets us check out these foods high in hyaluronic acid:

Sweet potatoes and potatoes are foods rich in hyaluronic acid

Sweet potatoes have a high content of hyaluronic acid. Likewise, the German potato contains hyaluronic acid, albeit lesser. Both are ideal side dishes every day.

Citrus fruits are foods rich in hyaluronic acid

Citrus fruits contain naringenin, which has an antioxidant effect; it traps free radicals. It also prevents the breakdown of hyaluronic acid in the body. Grapefruit, orange, or lemon, with their high content of naringenin, vitamin C, and magnesium, are superfoods for the skin and losing weight because if you are a fruit, you have less desire for sweets.

Bone broth and chicken soup are foods rich in hyaluronic acid

In modern cuisine, bone stock is rarely getting used. However, it contains a large amount of hyaluronic acid, increases the collagen level, provides our joints with necessary substances, and ensures healthy intestinal bacteria. Chicken soup, beef soup, in which bones, cartilage, and skin are getting cooked together, contains just as much natural hyaluronic. It is better to prefer meat with bones or add a few bones to a roast, and it also improves the taste and consistency of the gravy.

Soya are foods rich in hyaluronic acid

Researchers believe that estrogen in the body is beneficial for hyaluronic acid production. Soy products like tofu, tempeh, or edamame – all products made from soybeans – contain estrogen. Soy is one of the best sources of protein. Even as a convinced meat lover, you should include tofu in your diet from time to time. If you don’t like tofu, you can use soy milk instead. The vegetable milk has a nutty taste that goes well with any muesli.
